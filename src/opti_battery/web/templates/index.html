📊 Target vs. What We Actually Achieved
Honest scorecard. The "Insane Target" is the marketing dream. "Achieved" = the best real, already-known material our pipeline screened from the Materials Project database, with its computed (DFT / theoretical) property — not a lab-measured cell and not a new element. "Reality Gap" is what still stands between a database hit and a working battery.
Feature Insane Target Achieved (screened candidate) Reality Gap
⚡ Charging Speed 100% in < 60 s Li₃N — 2.08 Å Li hop (fast-ion conductor found) Short hop ≠ full-pack in 60 s. MW-scale power delivery + heat + Li plating still block it.
🔋 Capacity 5–10 days runtime Li₉S₃N — 5,029 mAh/g theoretical Theoretical gravimetric max. Real cells lose to cathode, packaging, S shuttling. Days only for low-draw devices.
♻️ Lifespan 10+ yrs / immortal LiThF₅ — −4.12 eV/atom formation (very stable) Thermodynamic stability ≠ cycle life. And LiThF₅ contains thorium (radioactive) — not shippable.
📐 Form Factor Transparent, card-thin LiS₄ — 0.21 g/cm³ (ultra-low density) Low density is one number; thin/transparent needs full device stack (current collectors, seals) not modeled here.
🛡️ Durability Bends & bulletproof LiAlB₁₄ — 382 GPa stiffness score High modulus = stiff, not flexible. Stiff + bendable are opposite. Bulletproof is a pack-level claim.
🔌 Passive Power Charge while walking NbAs — 119 C/m² piezo tensor Piezo harvest = µW–mW, trickle only. Won't meaningfully charge a phone. Contains arsenic.
Bottom line: the pipeline succeeded at what it's built for — it found real, computed candidate materials that top each metric in a database. It did not discover a new element, and it did not build or measure a battery. A working cell needs synthesis, electrode/interface engineering, and physical cycle testing — the "Reality Gap" column.
